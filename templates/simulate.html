Non-Ideal Effects: Interface & Roughness

Real devices never have perfectly sharp interfaces or identical periods. These are the two imperfections modelled by the simulator.

1. Graded interface — the "step-like" transition (Nsub, dtrans)

The refractive index does not jump abruptly from metal to etch. It is approximated as a staircase of Nsub sub-layers across the transition thickness dtrans.

Graded index n(z) — Nsub steps Ideal abrupt step

2. Etch-length roughness (σLe)

Fabrication disorder makes each period's etched length vary randomly around Le. The dashed grid marks the ideal uniform lattice, so the drift of the real teeth reveals the roughness.
